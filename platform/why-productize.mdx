## The Productization Formula

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Your IP">
    What do you do repeatedly that delivers consistent value? Look for:
    - Processes you've refined over multiple clients
    - Frameworks that consistently drive results
    - Methodologies unique to your approach
    - Data or insights you've accumulated

    <Tip>
      **Quick Test:** If you've done it successfully 3+ times, it's ready to productize.
    </Tip>

  </Step>

  <Step title="Extract the Pattern">
    Document the methodology that makes your service special:
    - Map the step-by-step process
    - Identify decision points and logic
    - Capture templates and frameworks
    - Define success metrics

    This becomes your product's core engine.

  </Step>

{" "}

<Step title="Build the Container">
  Create the delivery mechanism: - **Calculator/Estimator:** Turn your pricing
  model into a tool - **Assessment/Audit:** Package your evaluation process -
  **Dashboard/Reporter:** Visualize your insights - **Workflow/Automation:**
  Systematize your methodology
</Step>

  <Step title="Price for Value">
    Shift from hourly to outcome-based pricing:

    | Pricing Model | Example | Margin Impact |
    |--------------|---------|---------------|
    | Hourly Service | $150/hour | 20-30% margin |
    | Project-Based | $15,000 fixed | 30-40% margin |
    | Productized Tool | $1,500/month | 70-80% margin |
    | Platform Access | $500/seat/month | 85-95% margin |

  </Step>

  <Step title="Scale Distribution">
    One product, unlimited reach:
    - Self-service onboarding
    - Automated delivery
    - Built-in virality
    - Partner channels

    This is how you escape the revenue-per-headcount trap.

  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Objections (And Why They're Wrong)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Our work is too custom to productize" icon="puzzle-piece">
    **The Reality:** 80% of what you call custom is actually a pattern you haven't recognized yet.

    **The Solution:** Start with the 80% that's repeatable. The 20% that's truly custom becomes your premium service tier at 3x the price.

    **Example:** A branding agency thought every project was unique until they realized their discovery process, competitive analysis, and presentation formats were 90% identical across clients.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Clients won't pay for tools" icon="credit-card">
    **The Reality:** Your clients already pay for tools - they're called SaaS subscriptions.

    **Your Advantage:** Your tools have something generic tools don't:
    - Deep industry expertise
    - Client-specific context
    - Integrated methodology
    - Direct support channel

    **Proof:** Agencies charging $500-2000/month for specialized tools vs $50-200 for generic alternatives.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="We'll cannibalize our service revenue" icon="chart-pie">
    **The Data:** Every agency that's productized reports service revenue actually INCREASES.

    **Why It Happens:**
    - Tools qualify better clients
    - Products create upsell opportunities
    - Automation frees time for premium work
    - Platform lock-in increases lifetime value

    **Case Study:** Marketing agency saw 40% service revenue increase after launching assessment tool that pre-qualified enterprise clients.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="We don't have technical capabilities" icon="code">
    **The Truth:** You don't need to be technical to productize.

    **Your Options:**
    - Partner with technical co-founder
    - Use no-code platforms
    - Outsource development
    - Work with WithSeismic

    **Remember:** Your value is the IP, not the code. Focus on what you know, partner for what you don't.

  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="We don't have time to build products" icon="clock">
    **The Cost of Waiting:**
    - Competitors building moats
    - AI commoditizing services
    - Clients demanding self-service
    - Valuation staying flat
    - Best people leaving

    **The Math:** 3 months to build = 3 years of compound returns. You don't have time NOT to productize.

  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
